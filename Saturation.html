Career Saturation Cycles

5 Stages of Career Saturation & How It Works

Careers follow a predictable cycle where emerging fields start as lucrative goldmines due to scarcity, then attract masses, leading to oversupply, automation, and commoditization. Only niches survive long-term, restarting the cycle with new fields.

1. Emergence (0–5 years)

New field appears with few experts, driving high demand, salaries, and prestige.

2. Hype & Rush (5–15 years)

Media and society promote it; training surges, competition rises, but pay stays high.

3. Mass Adoption (15–25 years)

Millions enter; work standardizes, outsourcing begins, salaries plateau.

4. Automation & Commoditization (25–35 years)

Tech automates routines; oversupply and global competition crash pay, turning it into a rat race.

5. Niche Survival (35+ years)

Only specialists or cross-domain experts thrive; a new field emerges to restart the cycle.

Saturation in Historical Fields

Government Jobs (1950s–1970s)

1. Emergence (1950–1955): Post-independence scarcity; high stability in railways, PSUs, UPSC.
2. Hype & Rush (1955–1965): Society glorified "sarkari naukri"; families pushed kids into exams.
3. Mass Adoption (1965–1975): Population boom; millions compete via coaching centers.
4. Automation & Commoditization (1975–1985): Corruption, quotas, freezes; pay lags private sector.
5. Niche Survival (1985–present): IAS/IPS/IFS prestigious; rest stagnant.

Medicine (1960s–1980s)

1. Emergence (1960–1965): Few doctors; high demand, respect, earnings.
2. Hype & Rush (1965–1975): Families pushed into MBBS.
3. Mass Adoption (1975–1985): Private colleges surge supply, especially urban.
4. Automation & Commoditization (1985–1995): Diagnostics, pharma chains reduce general earnings.
5. Niche Survival (1995–present): Specialists (cardio, neuro) and abroad thrive.

Engineering (1970s–1990s)

1. Emergence (1970–1975): Nation-building roles in dams, ISRO.
2. Hype & Rush (1975–1985): Govt/families push BTech; colleges expand.
3. Mass Adoption (1985–1995): 1.5M annual grads; poor placements except IITs.
4. Automation & Commoditization (1995–2005): Routine work; "engineer = unemployed."
5. Niche Survival (2005–present): IIT/R&D specialists premium.

IT & Software (1980s–2000s)

1. Emergence (1980–1985): Outsourcing boom; dollar salaries at Infosys/TCS.
2. Hype & Rush (1985–1995): IT institutes (NIIT); parents push coding.
3. Mass Adoption (1995–2005): Millions in hubs; pay normalizes.
4. Automation & Commoditization (2005–2015): AI tools, outsourcing to Philippines; routine low pay.
5. Niche Survival (2015–present): Cloud/AI/cybersecurity high-paying.

MBA & Finance (1990s–2010s)

1. Emergence (1990–1995): Liberalization; demand for managers/bankers.
2. Hype & Rush (1995–2005): IIM packages explode; MBA craze.
3. Mass Adoption (2005–2015): Private colleges; lakhs enroll, low jobs.
4. Automation & Commoditization (2015–2025): Digitization, outsourcing routine work.
5. Niche Survival (2025–present): Top schools/global finance (VC/PE) lucrative.

Startups & Entrepreneurship (2010s–2020s)

1. Emergence (2010–2015): Flipkart/Ola; billionaire valuations.
2. Hype & Rush (2015–2020): Everyone launches apps; investor money flows.
3. Mass Adoption (2020–2025): Funding easy; lakhs join.
4. Automation & Commoditization (2025–2035): Funding winter; copy-paste failures.
5. Niche Survival (2035–future): Deeptech (AI/biotech/EV) potential.

NRI Abroad Dream (1980s–2000s)

1. Emergence (1980–1985): Few with foreign degrees; elite status, dollars.
2. Hype & Rush (1985–1995): Gulf/US migration; family dream.
3. Mass Adoption (1995–2005): Lakhs go yearly; diaspora explodes, costs eat earnings.
4. Automation & Commoditization (2005–2015): Oversupply, tight visas, stagnant wages.
5. Niche Survival (2015–present): Elite AI/biotech/VC roles abroad rich.

AI & Data: Current Status

AI is in Stage 2: Hype & Early Mass Adoption (2020–2025). Generative AI drives demand, but bootcamps create rush. Mass adoption by 2025–2030; commoditization by 2030–2040, saturating basic roles. Niches survive post-2040.

Safe Bets for 2025–2040

  • AI + Domain Hybrids (law, medicine, finance)
  • Robotics / Hardware AI (drones, biotech)
  • Climate Tech / Energy (green, carbon capture)
  • Longevity & Biotech (anti-aging, genetics)
  • Geo-strategy & Security (cyberwarfare, space)
