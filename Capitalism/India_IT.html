The 85% Capitalism Problem & IT Sector Evolution

What Is the 85% Capitalism Problem?

India’s high-capitalism environment prioritizes market forces—competition, efficiency, performance—over traditional safety nets like job security. Key traits:

  • Institutions no longer guarantee livelihoods.
  • Skills and differentiation outweigh credentials.
  • Workers face intense local and global competition.

Indian IT Sector: Then vs Now

1. From Golden Era to Automation

Then (2000s–2010s): Secure IT jobs, loyalty-based stability.

Now: Automation and AI eliminate entry-level tasks; companies demand immediate ROI.

2. Talent Surplus

Millions of engineers graduate annually, but IT jobs are scarce, leading to low wages and high entry barriers.

3. AI & Automation

AI erases traditional job roles, requiring AI-savvy specialists who can orchestrate technology.

4. Economic & Policy Shifts

Global recessions and labor reforms increase workplace exploitation, prioritizing cost over welfare.

5. Human Pressure

Automation, surplus talent, and deregulation create a high-stress, deliver-or-exit culture.

Implications for Professionals

  • Degrees alone don’t suffice—focus on AI-resistant skills (cloud, AI/ML, cybersecurity).
  • Stand out with projects and impact, not just credentials.
  • Security comes from adaptability, freelancing, and continuous learning.

Summary: Then vs Now

Factor Then (Golden Era) Now (85% Capitalism)
Job Security High, stable paths Low, performance-driven
Labor Supply Balanced Massive surplus
Role Requirements Generalist, process-driven Specialist, AI-savvy
Economic/Policy Predictable Tight budgets, deregulation
