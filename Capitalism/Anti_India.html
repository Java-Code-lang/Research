Historical Context: Wealth and Power Dynamics

A broad historical perspective highlights shifting global dynamics:

  • Ancient Era: Hindus were advanced, rich, and tolerant.
  • Medieval Era: Muslims gained wealth through conquest and trade.
  • Modern Era (1500–1800): Christians advanced via colonial loot, least tolerant.
  • 19th–21st Century: Christians (West) richest, Jews influential, Hindus resurging, Muslims geopolitically significant but lagging in innovation.

Summary of Wealth Origins

  • Jews: Knowledge, literacy, finance, science.
  • Christians: Colonial loot + Industrial Revolution.
  • Muslims: Medieval trade/loot + modern oil wealth.

Why Hindus Were Targeted

  • Non-converting: Didn’t spread aggressively, seen as weak.
  • Wealth: India was the world’s richest land until 1700.
  • Idol Worship: Branded as “pagan” by Abrahamic faiths.
  • Resilience: Survived centuries of pressure without mass conversion.
  • Tolerance: Accepted all faiths, but others didn’t reciprocate.

Hindus were targeted due to their wealth, tolerance, and refusal to convert, frustrating exclusivist religions.
