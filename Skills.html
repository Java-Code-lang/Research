Trends Over the Years

  • 1999–2005: Very few “good” grads; IT industry was small. Most were average/bad.
  • 2010–2015: Rise of outsourcing → more “average” grads finding work.
  • Post-2018: AI/ML and startup boom → larger number of “good” grads emerging; still ~35% disengaged.

College Tier Insights

Top Skills (1-4)

Digital marketing, financial literacy, e-commerce, and entrepreneurship are most in-demand due to their direct impact on forex earnings (USD/EUR markets), low entry barriers (₹0-5 lakh), and ability to address small business failure causes (cash flow, digital presence, competition). They align with India’s digital and export growth and are accessible to CS grads (25% job-ready for tech integration; 40% for non-tech pivots).

Mid-Tier Skills (5-8)

Data analysis, communication, trade, and customer experience support scalability and global reach, critical for 5-10% currency exchange success and 20% business survival. They require moderate expertise but offer strong returns in competitive markets.

Lower-Tier Skills (9-12)

Supply chain, blockchain, sustainability, and compliance are valuable but niche or riskier. Blockchain is high-risk (80% crypto failure), while sustainability and compliance are less immediate for beginners, though growing in relevance.

Saturated Fields in 2025

  1. Basic Software Development (Entry-Level Coding)
  2. Traditional Web Development (Basic HTML/CSS/JS)
  3. General IT Support (Basic Helpdesk/Tech Support)
  4. General Construction Labor
  5. Basic Hospitality and Housekeeping
  6. Generic Digital Marketing (Basic SEO/Content Writing)
  7. Traditional Retail Sales Assistance

Why These Fields Are Saturated

  • Oversupply of Workers: India’s 3.8 crore CS grads (1999–2025), with 75% lacking advanced skills, flood entry-level tech and manpower roles.
  • Automation and AI: Tools like no-code platforms, AI content generators, and chatbots reduce demand for basic coding, web development, and digital marketing.
  • Global Competition: In stronger-currency countries, Indian workers compete with local and other low-cost labor, suppressing wages in manpower roles.
  • Low Differentiation: Basic skills lack the specialization needed for high-paying jobs in USD/EUR markets.
  • High Failure Risk: Fields like e-commerce (80% failure) and IT services (63%) face poor market fit.

Demand Drivers: Aging populations, tech booms, and infrastructure projects create shortages.

Monetization Modes: Remote/Freelancing, On-Site Jobs, Small Business Tie-In.

CS Graduate Fit: Good programmers excel in high-end tech; average/bad can pivot to data analysis or digital marketing.
